Abstract


The nexus of high resolution, multispectral imagery; Airborne Lidar; and vector-based fire modelling software produces high fidelity simulations of wildfire transmission in the WUI. The approach described in this paper outlines procedural and model-based solutions for generating localized, prescriptive changes to the form, structure, and composition of fuels in small urban, mediterranean communities. Using the communities of Orinda, California and La Reina, Chile, this research will highlight methodologies for data collection, processing, accuracy assessment, community engagement, and site specific fuel modeling. The aim of this research is to inform a comprehensive fire mitigation strategy for Orinda and Le Reina with universal application to Mediterranean climates around the world. We hope that this body of work will continue to foster collaboration between researchers in the field as we collectively improve our modeling methodologies and mitigation strategies to protect our urban fringe communities from fire risk.
